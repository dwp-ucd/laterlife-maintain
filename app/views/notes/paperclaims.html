Iteration 4

Date of design
July 2020
Designers
Emily Wilkinson and Clare Robinson

Scenario

  • Design for when a paper claims are in their own queue (need research)
  • Agent has ability to choose whether the claim can be complete on GET or not

Business requirements

  • When a correspondence comes in (proof of address) agent creates a record for the claim and puts a note in CAM to help signify that a claim was being handled in GySP
  • Agent needs to have the ability to choose whether a claim can be processed on GET, reasons for this are:
    - if the scanned in form is illegible
    - an old rules BR1
    - signed by a PAB or a CAB
    - requesting alternative contact formats

Research notes

  • Discovery research with 3 experienced SPOTT agents, agents were happy to change their way of working and have paper claims in one queue along with other tasks

Iteration 2 - MVP

benton park view testing
Date of design
June 2020
Designers
Emily Wilkinson and Clare Robinson

Scenario

  • Widowed claimant - asking for date the spouse died and date of marriage
  • Claimant has lived or worked in 4 different countries

Business requirements

  • No change links for dob and address
  • No signed form question
  • Taken out question about alternative format
  • Some claimants might have an NI number or social security number for that country if they have not worked there

Design pattern changes

  • New navigation bar
  • New design for find someone page
  • Changed 'citizen' to 'claimant' for consistency throughout agent service
  • Design change for navigating back to find someone

Iterations based on previous research

  • Took out radio buttons for lived or worked abroad questions and the ability to add multiple times they lived or worked abroad because there is nowhere to enter this information on the BR1 form
  • Moved NI number input out of radio buttons for worked abroad as it's own seperate field
  • Lived and worked abroad - took out radio reveals as some agents answered 'no' to 'do you know your National Insurance number?' rather than 'did you work here?'
  • Check details page - taken out DOB and address as it is not possible for an agent to change these for MVP
  • Check and change design for lived and worked abroad - use radio button for extra question

Iteration 1

tyne view park research
Date of design
February 2020
Designers
Emily Wilkinson and Clare Robinson

Scenario

  • ‘Happy path’ scenario - citizen’s details validated
  • Citizen needs alternative contact formats, is married, has lived and worked abroad

Research notes

  • Agents preferred how the new BR1 form matched the ordering of questions in the prototype, as opposed to how it works now in CAM
  • Some agents accidentally answered the worked abroad question wrong, because they were answering no to the NI number question
  • Some agents mentioned that if a citizen is divorced or widowed we need to know their date of marriage
  • Overall the prototype tested well with the BR1 form
